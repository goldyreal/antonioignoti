The War Poets

When the First world war broke out, thousands of men volunteered for military service. The reaction to war through 3 different stages:
1. The patriotic enthusiasm that led many to enlist (Brooke)
2. The realization that the War was a lie. The anger came (Sassoon).
3. The poetry gives voice to compassion and it is a sort of elegy for the young soldiers who must be admired and loved.
The War Poets took part in the war and managed to represent the modern times in a realistic way. Shell shock is a term coined during the first world war to describe the type of post traumatic stress disorder that many soldiers were with. It is a reaction to the intensity of the bombs and fighting that produced a sense of panic, inability to reason, sleep, walk or talk.
